About Austin

Settled in the early 1830s, and Austin was officialy incorporated as a city and established as the capitol of Texas when Texas fought and won its independence from Mexico. As the capitol of Texas, Austin is home of the Texas state government, and an extremely popular tourist destination for its music and film festivals, as well as a robust economy and vibrant green spaces.

Austin is classified as an urban city, boasting a population of roughly 1 million. With a median household income of $86,556, Austin is a popular destination to live for young professionals. Austin boasts a healthy economy in the technology, business, and finance. Home to headquarters or regional offices for tech giants such as AMD, Amazon, Tesla, Apple, Facebook, TI (Texas Instruments), and Oracle, Austin is the leading city in Texas for people seeking jobs in the tech industry.

Austin is a popular tourist destination, known for hosting music and film festivals ACL (Austin City Limits) and SXSW (South By Southwest). An active night life can also be found in Austin neighborhoods Downtown 6th Street, East 6th, Rainey Street, South Congress, and The Domain.
